Detection Pipeline

How Fake News is Detected

When you submit a news article, it passes through a 5-step pipeline before a verdict is returned.

1
Text Cleaning
Your input is converted to lowercase, punctuation and numbers are removed, and common words like "the", "is", "a" (stopwords) are filtered out. Only meaningful words remain.
"Trump ARRESTED!" → "trump arrested"
2
Model 1 — Logistic Regression + TF-IDF
TF-IDF converts your text into 10,000 numbers — one for each word in the vocabulary. Words that appear frequently in fake news get higher scores. Logistic Regression then predicts REAL or FAKE based on these numbers.
text → 10,000 numbers → REAL/FAKE + confidence %
3
Model 2 — LSTM Deep Learning
The LSTM neural network reads your text word-by-word, understanding the sequence and context — just like a human reads. Each word is mapped to 64 numbers (embeddings) that capture its meaning, and the LSTM processes them in order to detect patterns unique to fake news.
text → word sequences → neural network → REAL/FAKE + confidence %
4
Ensemble Verdict
Both model results are compared. If both agree, the verdict is shown with high confidence. If they disagree, an "Uncertain" result is shown and manual verification is recommended.
LR=FAKE + LSTM=FAKE → High confidence FAKE
5
LIME Word Influence Analysis
After the verdict, LIME (Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations) analyses which specific words in your article pushed the model towards FAKE or REAL. Red bars show words that indicate fake news, green bars show words that indicate real news — giving you a clear reason behind every decision.
verdict → LIME → top influencing words with weights
Explainable AI

Why LIME? — Understanding the Model's Decision

A high accuracy score alone is not enough for a trustworthy AI system. The model must also be able to explain why it made a decision. This is called Explainable AI (XAI).

LIME — Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations — works by creating hundreds of slightly modified versions of your input text and observing how the model's prediction changes. Words that cause the biggest change in prediction are the most influential.

1
LIME creates 500 text variations
It randomly removes words from your article to create 500 slightly different versions, then runs each through the model to see how the prediction changes.
2
Measures each word's influence
Words whose removal causes the biggest drop in FAKE confidence are the strongest fake news indicators. Words whose removal causes a drop in REAL confidence are real news indicators.
3
Top 5 words per side shown
The top 5 words pushing towards FAKE and top 5 pushing towards REAL are displayed with animated progress bars showing their relative influence strength.

The Science

Why Fake News Has Detectable Patterns

!
Sensational Language
Fake news uses emotionally charged, exaggerated words — "SHOCKING", "EXPOSED", "BREAKING" — more frequently than real journalism, which prefers neutral factual language.
!
Lack of Verified Sources
Real news articles frequently reference named journalists, wire services (Reuters, AP), or official statements. Fake news tends to use vague sourcing like "sources say" or "insiders claim".
✓
Consistent Writing Style
Legitimate news follows journalistic conventions — inverted pyramid structure, quotes, bylines. Our models learned these stylistic differences across 121,000+ articles.
✓
Topic and Word Patterns
Certain topics, names, and word combinations appear disproportionately in fake news. TF-IDF quantifies these patterns across 10,000 vocabulary words, giving the model a statistical fingerprint for fake vs real. LIME then reveals exactly which of these words influenced each individual prediction.
